Sites to explore:

Hawa Mahal (Jaipur)

The Hawa Mahal, built in 1799, is a stunning example of Rajput architecture designed to let royal ladies observe street festivities.

Amber Fort (Jaipur)

Amber Fort is a majestic fort known for its artistic Hindu-style elements, offering stunning views of Maota Lake.

Taj Mahal (Aagra)

The Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World, was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Aagra Fort (Aagra)

Agra Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage site, showcases the grandeur of Mughal architecture and served as a royal residence.

Red Fort (Delhi)

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, was commissioned for construction by Emperor Shah Jahan on May 12, 1639.

Qutub Minar (Delhi)

The Qutub Minar is a towering minaret located in Delhi, India, standing at 73 meters (240 feet). It was constructed in 1193 by Qutb-ud-Din Aibak, the first ruler of the Delhi Sultanate

Humayun's Tomb (Delhi)

Humayun's Tomb was commissioned by Humayun's wife, Empress Bega Begum, and completed in 1572, setting a precedent for the Taj Mahal

India Gate (Delhi)

India Gate, a war memorial built to honor the soldiers of the British Indian Army who died during World War I. The monument stands 42 meters tall and is a symbol of India's sacrifice and patriotism.
